Your Questions Answered

Browse through our comprehensive FAQ section to find detailed information about the submission process, editorial guidelines, and journal policies.

Yes, the SIES Journal of Multidisciplinary Research and Innovation is a double-blind peer-reviewed, multi-disciplinary journal. It is also ISSN certified (3048-9768 Online) and follows rigorous academic standards for publication.
All manuscripts must strictly follow the guidelines of the APA 7th Edition style for citations, references, and formatting. Please ensure your manuscript adheres to these standards before submission.
The double-blind peer-review process typically takes between 8 to 10 weeks from the date of submission. Authors are notified promptly of the decision along with reviewer comments and feedback.
We accept manuscripts in PDF and Microsoft Word (.docx) formats. Please ensure your manuscript is anonymized for the double-blind review process and follows the journal's formatting guidelines.
Research articles should be between 3,000-8,000 words including references and appendices. Review articles may extend up to 10,000 words. The abstract should be 150-250 words.
Currently, a publication fee applies for accepted articles. The Article Processing Charge (APC) covers open-access publication, DOI assignment, and online hosting. For the current fee amount, waiver policies, and payment instructions, please see Submission Guidelines or contact journal@sies.edu.in.
The journal welcomes submissions from social sciences, humanities, management studies, education, library science, environmental studies, and other interdisciplinary research areas that contribute to multidisciplinary knowledge.
Yes, we welcome submissions from researchers worldwide. The journal has an international scope and encourages submissions from scholars across different countries and cultural contexts.
